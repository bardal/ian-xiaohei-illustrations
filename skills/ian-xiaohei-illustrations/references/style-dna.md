# Style DNA

## One-liner

Pure white, minimalist, hand-drawn, spacious, restrained, absurd, product-sketch feeling, structured but never instructional.

Like a quick explanation sketch drawn on a blank sheet of paper by someone who has spent years working with AI, product, design, and developer tools.

## Must-haves

- 16:9 horizontal article illustration.
- Pure white background: no off-white, warm grey, paper texture, gradients, shadows, noise, or vintage paper feel.
- Black hand-drawn line art as the primary medium: thin lines, slight wobble, not mechanical, not vector-clean, not thick-stroked.
- Lots of white space: main subject covers roughly 40%-60% of the canvas, at least 35% blank, ideally one large quiet area.
- Sparse handwritten annotations: at most 5-8 labels, each as short as possible.
- One image communicates only one core action, structure, state, or metaphor.
- Let structure emerge naturally; do not write the structure type name on the image.

## Colors

- Black: main line art, characters, borders, structure, primary text, main objects.
- Red: key annotations, problems, emotional moments, critical warnings, results.
- Orange: main flow, paths, arrows, automation direction, A-to-B movement.
- Blue: secondary notes, internal mental state, system state, second-layer explanation, AI/assistant/automation hints.

Blue is not required in every image. Use color sparingly — less is better.

## Absolute don'ts

- No commercial illustration.
- No PPT infographic.
- No formal flowchart.
- No course slide or lecture material.
- No cute cartoon poster.
- No children's illustration.
- No complex architecture diagram.
- No polished flat-design illustration.
- No tech-UI aesthetic.
- No real app screenshots.
- No complex backgrounds, gradients, shadows, or textures.
- Do not explain every node.
- Do not write a type label ("Workflow / System Architecture / Common Pitfalls / Roadmap") in the top-left corner.

## Aesthetic direction

Aim for: absurd, creative, interesting, clean, and imaginative.

Avoid: cute, childish, overly complex, rigid.
